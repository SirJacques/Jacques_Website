Current Work

24 GHz Radar for Gesture Sensing

My current research, at the university of Stellenbosch, is oriented into develping a 24 GHz RADAR system with sufficient doppler resolution for gesture sensing applications.

My current supervisors are Prof. JB DeSwardt and Dr. Paul Van der Merwe, as part of the EMACS research group.

About

About Me

I am a Masters student at the university of Stellenbosch. If I'm not working in the lab you'll most likely find me on the tennis court or out surfing the vast beautiful coastline of South Africa.

Music is also something which has always been a large part of my life. I received classical piano lessons under the watchful eye of Lorraine Lategan and, later, Angela Lubbe. Now my music is much more guitar orientated and I'm currently working on my debut album with Pierre Stemmett scheduled for release in September 2020.

Skills

I hold a degree in Electrical & Electronic Engineering from Stellenbosch University with a focus in RF engineering. Coupled resonator filter design and microwave network design are my current strengths.

I also have experience developing software for digital signal processing and have developed embedded systems using the STM and ESP32 micro-controllers. Apart from that, I have designed and constructed a working 25 watt class A/B audio amplifier and a 10 GHz integrated coupled resonator filter.
